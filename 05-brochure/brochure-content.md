## Inside Center Panel: The Climate Challenge

### Why Climate Matters for the Netherlands

- **26% below sea level** — existential flood risk requiring continuous investment
- **9 tonnes CO2 per capita** — above EU average of 7.5 tonnes
- **Nitrogen crisis** — halted construction and worsened housing shortage
- **14,000+ projects** on grid waiting lists (9 GW capacity)
- **Groningen gas field closing** — must replace with clean energy
- **€10+ billion** in annual adaptation costs by 2050 without action

### Emissions by Sector
- Energy: 40%
- Agriculture: 15%
- Transport: 15%
- Industry: 15%
- Buildings: 10%

## Inside Right Panel: Our Policy Recommendations

### Policy 1: Grid Modernization (€10B, 2027-2031)
- Expand transmission capacity by 10+ GW
- Deploy 5 GWh storage
- Smart grid technology
- Priority: Port of Rotterdam hydrogen connections

### Policy 2: Agricultural Transition (€5B, 2027-2033)
- **Pathway A:** Voluntary buyout (120% value)
- **Pathway B:** Sustainable transition grants (50%)
- **Pathway C:** Farm diversification (solar, tourism)
- Target: 10,500 farms, 50,000 hectares to nature

### Policy 3: Green Skills (€2B, 2027-2031)
- 50,000 workers reskilled
- 80% wage during training
- Employer tax credits
- Focus: Groningen, farming provinces

### Euro Impact
*"Euro membership enables access to €500B+ green bond market, €4B in EU climate funds, and the unified EU ETS carbon price."*

## Back Cover: Key Terms

**EU ETS:** European Emissions Trading System — cap-and-trade system for carbon emissions

**CBAM:** Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism — applies carbon price to imports

**Externality:** Cost or benefit of economic activity borne by third parties (e.g., pollution)

**Green Bond:** Debt instrument funding environmentally sustainable projects

**Just Transition:** Ensuring fair treatment for workers in the transition to green economy
